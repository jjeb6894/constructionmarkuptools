## Non-Negotiable Principles

1. Write for construction professionals first, not for search engines first.
2. Demonstrate practical experience: workflows, compatibility checks, file-import steps, licence clarity, support expectations, and trade-specific use cases.
3. Be accurate about product status. Do not invent symbol counts, customer reviews, ratings, endorsements, compatibility guarantees, or prices.
4. Do not copy, paraphrase closely, or imitate competitor text, page structure, image choices, symbol designs, downloads, or sales claims.
5. Use competitor research only as market intelligence: category coverage, visible pricing signals, common questions, trust patterns, and gaps.
6. Make the page useful even if the reader never buys: explain what the tool set is, who it is for, how it works, what to check before purchase, and common mistakes.
7. Use clear UK English. Avoid hype, filler, keyword stuffing, and generic AI-sounding claims.
8. Include the Bluebeam disclaimer on every commercial or support page:
   `Bluebeam and Revu are trademarks of their respective owners. Construction Markup Tools is not affiliated with or endorsed by Bluebeam, Inc.`

## Page-Level Requirements

Every product or money page should include:

- Clear title tag and H1 aligned with one primary search intent.
- Short opening section that identifies the buyer, the problem, and the outcome.
- What's included, using planned original product data only.
- Who this is for.
- How it works in Bluebeam Revu.
- Compatibility section stating that Bluebeam Revu is required and not included.
- Licence and usage section.
- Common use cases.
- Free sample CTA.
- Bundle or next-step CTA.
- Internal links to compatibility, licence, support, FAQ, and related trade pages.
- Original FAQs based on buyer doubts and workflow questions.
- Schema notes for Product, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, and HowTo where relevant.

## Technical SEO Requirements

- One indexable URL per intent.
- Descriptive title and meta description.
- Logical headings in order.
- No hidden text or misleading structured data.
- Schema must represent visible page content.
- Page should be fast, mobile-friendly, and easy to scan.
- Avoid intrusive popups, excessive scripts, and heavy media.
- Add sitemap and robots.txt.
- Keep internal links natural and useful.

## AI Content Rules

Google does not ban AI-assisted content solely because it is AI-generated, but the output must be helpful, reliable, and created for people. Therefore:

- AI drafts must be reviewed.
- AI must not invent facts.
- AI must not use scraped competitor wording as source text for rewriting.
- AI must produce original commercial explanations based on structured facts and product strategy.
- Every factual market insight must remain traceable to source URLs in internal reports.

## Voice

Practical, specific, calm, and trade-aware. The reader should feel the page was written by someone who understands construction documentation, drawing reviews, Bluebeam Revu workflows, and the difference between a generic symbol list and a usable Tool Chest workflow.
